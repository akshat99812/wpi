Wind finance macro signals▾
  • Wind auction tariff trajectory. SECI weighted clearing ₹3.05/kWh FY24 — re-rated up from the ₹2.43/kWh SECI-I low (Feb 2017) as IEC Class III machines, 140 m+ towers and BoP truth-up worked through. Floor signal ₹2.95–3.10/kWh; sponsors pricing below this risk thin DSCRs.
  • Project debt — IREDA / REC / PFC. AAA wind sponsors quoted 8.75–9.50% at 18-yr tenor; unrated SPVs ~100 bps over. DSRA 2 quarters, 1-month WC, equated annuity amortisation are Indian-wind covenant standard.
  • Sec 115BAA + Sec 32(1) 40% WDV. Concessional 22% (effective 25.168% with surcharge + cess) plus 40% WDV depreciation on wind devices delivers a ~10-year tax shield — worth 250–350 bps of post-tax equity IRR uplift vs full-tax assumption.
  • FDRE / RTC premium. Bundled wind+solar+BESS clearing ₹4.20–5.50/kWh — ~₹1.0–1.5/kWh premium over vanilla wind PPAs in exchange for 80–85% annual CUF firmness; wind anchors the night block.
  • Repowering brownfield pipeline. ~25 GW of pre-2010 sub-2 MW machines on India's best wind sites; turnkey ₹6.5–8 Cr/MW (vs ₹8.5–9.5 greenfield) since land + evacuation already exist — 1.6–2.4× energy uplift on the same site.
  • ALMM-II for WTGs. Approved-list regime narrows the eligible OEM pool to ~9 nameplates — tier-1 turbine vendors command a financing premium; ~70–85% localisation depth mutes INR FX exposure on imported nacelles/blades.
  • Group captive uplift. +₹0.40–0.70/kWh on PPA tariff via 26% equity in the SPV with consumption-share alignment — the most accretive wind off-take route for C&I credit in 2024-25.

Bankability verdict

Indicatively bankable — post-tax equity IRR 14.1% and avg DSCR 1.46× clear typical Indian wind IPP debt covenants. Validate with mast data and DD before close.
1. Generation & degradation

Yearly net energy yield is built up from nameplate capacity, hours, plant load factor and standard wind-IPP loss assumptions, then degraded year-on-year.

Gross MWhY(n) = CapacityMW × 8760 × PLF Net MWhY(n) = Gross × (1 − 0.005aux) × (1 − 0.005deg)(n−1) Generation MUY(n) = Net MWhY(n) / 1000

Auxiliary consumption (0.5 % of gross) reflects met-mast, SCADA and grid-condition draw. Degradation 0.5 %/yr is the standard NIWE / IEC reference for utility-scale wind.

2. Revenue (flat tariff)

Indian wind PPAs are typically flat — no escalation. Revenue is generation × tariff with a unit conversion to crores.

RevenueY(n) [Cr] = Net MWhY(n) × Tariff₹/kWh × 1000 / 10⁷
3. Operating costs & EBITDA

O&M is escalated 5 %/yr (FSA / AMC norm); insurance is 0.40 % of CapEx with 3 %/yr escalation.

O&MY(n) [Cr] = (Capacity × O&ML/MW/yr) / 100 × 1.05(n−1) InsuranceY(n) [Cr] = Turnkey × 0.0040 × 1.03(n−1) EBITDAY(n) = Revenue − O&M − Insurance
4. CapEx structure (turnkey = WTG + BoP)

The model treats turnkey CapEx as the sum of the WTG package and Balance-of-Plant, not WTG alone — which mirrors how Indian wind IPPs actually price projects.

TurnkeyCr/MW = WTG + BoP (≈ ₹8.5–9.5 Cr/MW) Total CapExCr = Capacity × Turnkey DebtCr = Total × Debt% Equityhard = Total − Debt Working Capital = RevenueY1 / 12 (one-month cycle) Equityincl WC = Equityhard + WC
5. Debt service — equated annuity, split into interest & principal

Long-tenor wind-IPP debt is amortised on an equated annual instalment (similar to a mortgage). The fixed annuity is split each year — interest accrues on the outstanding balance, the rest pays down principal.

Annuity = Debt × r × (1+r)N / [(1+r)N − 1] InterestY(n) = OutstandingY(n−1) × r PrincipalY(n) = Annuity − InterestY(n) OutstandingY(n) = OutstandingY(n−1) − PrincipalY(n) DSCRY(n) = EBITDAY(n) / Annuity (pre-tax — covenant standard)
6. Depreciation — Sec 32(1) Wind WDV 40 %

Indian Income-Tax Rules (Block of Assets, Sec 32(1)) prescribe 40 % WDV depreciation for wind energy devices post-2017 (cut from 80 %). Depreciation is fully exhausted by ~Year 10 — beyond which the project becomes a tax-cash payer.

DepY(n) = WDVY(n−1) × 0.40 WDVY(n) = WDVY(n−1) − DepY(n) WDVY0 = Total CapEx
7. Taxation — Sec 115BAA effective 25.168 %

Most renewable IPPs opt into the concessional Section 115BAA regime — flat 22 % rate that grosses up to 25.168 % effective with surcharge and cess. MAT does not apply under 115BAA, but accelerated depreciation and most other deductions are forfeited.

Effective tax = 22% × (1 + 10% surcharge) × (1 + 4% cess) = 0.22 × 1.10 × 1.04 = 0.25168 ⇒ 25.168 % TaxableunlevY(n) = max(0, EBITDAY(n) − DepY(n)) TaxablelevY(n) = max(0, EBITDAY(n) − DepY(n) − InterestY(n)) TaxY(n) = Taxable × 0.25168
8. Cashflows — unlevered & levered, post-tax

Two parallel cashflow streams are built so the verdict shows both project economics (independent of capital structure) and equity returns (after debt service).

Project CFY(n) = EBITDA − Taxunlev (unlevered, post-tax) Equity CFY(n) = EBITDA − Annuity − Taxlev (levered, post-tax) at t = 0: Project CF0 = − Total CapEx − WC Equity CF0 = − Equityincl WC
9. Year-25 terminal — salvage, cap-gains, WC release

Salvage value is taken at 5 % of CapEx at year 25 (industry rule of thumb for wind). Capital gains are taxed on the excess of salvage over remaining WDV, and the working capital block is released back to equity.

Salvage = Total CapEx × 0.05 Cap-gains tax = max(0, Salvage − WDVY25) × 0.25168 Terminal CF = Salvage − Cap-gains tax + WCrecovered Project CFY25 += Terminal CF Equity CFY25 += Terminal CF
10. NPV & IRR — Newton-Raphson with bisection fallback

NPV is the standard discounted cashflow sum (with t = 0 not discounted). IRR is solved iteratively, with a guarded numerical method:

NPV(r, CF) = Σᵢ CF[i] / (1 + r)i (i = 0, 1, …, 25) IRR seeded at r = 10 %; Newton-Raphson update: rk+1 = rk − f(rk) / f′(rk) where f(r) = NPV(r, CF) Falls back to bisection over [−0.95, 5] if Newton diverges. Project IRR → solved on Project CF stream (unlevered, post-tax) Equity IRR → solved on Equity CF stream (levered, post-tax)
11. Bankability decision rule

The verdict combines a return test and a coverage test — both must clear. This mirrors how Indian wind IPP debt committees actually screen projects.

  • Bankable — post-tax equity IRR ≥ 13 % AND average DSCR ≥ 1.30 ×
  • Marginal — post-tax equity IRR ≥ 11 % AND average DSCR ≥ 1.15 × (clearable with credit enhancement, sponsor guarantee or tariff floor)
  • Sub-bankable — fails either threshold; tariff, leverage, PLF or O&M assumptions need revision
12. Worked example — current base case

Inputs: 100 MW · WTG ₹6.50 + BoP ₹2.50 = ₹9.0 Cr/MW turnkey · 75 % debt @ 9.0 % over 18 yrs · Tariff ₹4.20/kWh · PLF 37 % · O&M ₹8.0 L/MW/yr.

CapEx structure: Total ₹900 Cr · Debt ₹675 Cr · Equity (hard) ₹225 Cr · WC ₹11.3 Cr (= 135.4 / 12) · Equity (incl WC) ₹236 Cr.

Annuity: 675 × 0.09 × 1.0918 / (1.0918 − 1) = 675 × 0.1142 = ₹77.1 Cr/yr for 18 yrs.

Y1 P&L: Gen 322.5 MU · Rev ₹135.4 Cr · O&M ₹8.0 Cr · Insurance ₹3.6 Cr · EBITDA ₹123.8 Cr (91.4 % margin) · DSCRY1 = 123.8 / 77.1 = 1.61 ×.

Tax shield: Y1 dep = 900 × 0.40 = ₹360 Cr — exceeds EBITDA, so zero tax in early years. Depreciation exhausts around Y10, after which 25.168 % tax kicks in on full taxable income.

Returns: Project IRR 9.6 % (post-tax, unlevered) · Equity IRR 14.1 % (post-tax, levered) · Payback 8.7 yrs · 25-yr ROI 295 % · Avg DSCR 1.46 × · Min DSCR 1.30 ×.

Verdict: equity IRR 14.1 % > 13 % bar AND DSCR 1.46 × > 1.30 covenant ⇒ Indicatively bankable.

1. Resource layer — atlases & reanalysis

Wind-resource numbers cited above are taken from NIWE's 150 m wind atlas (the official Indian reference, updated 2024) and cross-checked against DTU/IRENA Global Wind Atlas v3 at 150 m. The two diverge by 0.3–0.6 m/s in coastal-shear sites and the higher-confidence number is preferred.

  • Spatial resolution. NIWE atlas at 250 m × 250 m grid; GWA at ~250 m bilinear-resampled to 100/150/200 m.
  • Period. NIWE long-term reanalysis 2008–2023; GWA ERA5 1991–2020 climatology.
  • Hub-height extrapolation. Power-law shear with α tuned per LiDAR campaign where available, defaulting to α = 0.14 (terrain-flat) / 0.20 (complex terrain).
2. Generation statistics — P50 / P75 / P90

India project finance increasingly underwrites at P75 (or P90) energy yields, not P50. The conversion is done via the lognormal yield distribution implied by site-specific uncertainty.

Pn (MWh) = P50 × exp(−zn × σ) where: σ = √(σ²met + σ²shear + σ²variability + σ²future) z50=0, z75=0.674, z90=1.282 Typical Indian onshore σ stack: σmet ≈ 4–6 % σshear ≈ 3–5 % σvariability ≈ 4–6 % σfuture ≈ 3–5 % → combined σ ≈ 8–11 % ⇒ P50 → P90 spread ≈ 10–14 %
3. Plant Load Factor — convention
PLF (%) = Net energy (MWh) / [Capacity (MW) × 8760] × 100 Net energy = Gross MWh × (1 − Aux%) × (1 − Wake%) × (1 − Avail%) × (1 − Curtailment%)

India national average PLF (~24 %) is the fleet-weighted figure including the long tail of pre-2010 sub-2 MW machines on degraded sites. The top-decile PLF (38–42 %) reflects new 4 MW-class machines on Class I sites with modern wake-optimised layouts.

4. LCOE — methodology
LCOE (₹/kWh) = (Σ Costst / (1+r)t) / (Σ Energyt / (1+r)t) where: Costst = CapEx0 + O&Mt + Insurancet + Taxt + WCt Energyt = Net MWh × (1 − degradation)t−1 r = post-tax WACC (typ 8.5–10 %)

Quoted Indian wind LCOE band of ₹2.40–3.00/kWh uses 8.5 Cr/MW turnkey, 75/25 D/E, 9.0 % cost of debt, 13–15 % cost of equity, 25 yrs PPA, 33–37 % PLF, 5 % salvage. Variants pull this band higher (offshore: ₹6–8) or lower (best-in-class onshore: ₹2.20).

5. Tariff aggregation — capacity-weighted

Auction-clearing tariffs cited above are capacity-weighted across winning bids, not arithmetic means.

τ̄ = Σ (τi × MWi) / Σ MWi

This avoids small "edge-of-tender" lots distorting the headline number. State DISCOM PPAs are reported as a band because state-level disclosure varies (TANGEDCO, GUVNL, KPTCL publish; some state utilities don't).

6. Curtailment — measurement

Curtailment is reported as % of feasible energy lost, not % of nameplate. Two definitions co-exist; we use the SLDC-side measurement (commercial curtailment) because that's what flows into deviation settlement.

Curtailment % = (Escheduled − Edelivered) / Escheduled × 100
7. Tax & depreciation regime

For finance-engine cross-reference: most renewable IPPs operate under Sec 115BAA (concessional 22 %, effective 25.168 % with surcharge + cess) with Sec 32(1) Block 8 (Wind energy devices) at 40 % WDV depreciation (cut from 80 % post-Apr 2017). This combination produces a 10-year tax shield on most projects.

8. Repowering economics
Energy uplift = (PLFnew × MWnew) / (PLFold × MWold) Typical: 10 × 0.6 MW @ 18 % PLF → 1 × 4.0 MW @ 38 % PLF = (0.38 × 4.0) / (0.18 × 6.0) = 1.41× (with FEWER turbines)
9. Offshore-onshore tariff gap

Offshore wind tariffs sit ₹2.50–4.50/kWh above onshore comparable Indian sites, driven by foundation cost (~30–40 % of project cost), substation, cable burial and maintenance vessel logistics. VGF support (₹6,853 Cr for 1 GW Gujarat block) bridges this for the first commercial scale-up.

10. Forecasting & DSM penalties
If |Forecast − Actual| / Sched ≤ 15% → no DSM penalty If 15–25 % → paise/kWh slab 1 If 25–35 % → paise/kWh slab 2 (steeper) If > 35 % → curtailment + max paise/kWh slab

State-of-the-art numerical weather prediction (NWP) + machine-learning ensemble forecasters routinely hit 8–13 % MAPE on day-ahead Indian wind. RE-MC (Renewable Energy Management Centre) at POSOCO coordinates the central layer.

11. Limitations — what's not in this bench
  • Site-level micrositing. CFD wake models (WAsP, WindSim, OpenFOAM-SOWFA) need turbine coordinates; out of scope for a bench-level reference.
  • Counterparty / offtake risk. DISCOM payment delays, tariff renegotiation history, sovereign-rating sensitivity — needs a separate credit-bureau layer.
  • Construction risk. Land aggregation timelines, RoW for evacuation, permit cycles — modelled in due diligence but not aggregated here.
  • Forecast accuracy & merchant exposure. If the project sells uncontracted energy, merchant-price scenarios (DAM / RTM / GDAM) need to be modelled in the Finance Engine, not here.
